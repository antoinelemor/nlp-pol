# Annotation Prompt — Political Resignation Speech Analysis (Legault Resignation, January 2026)

## Context
You are annotating sentences from the resignation speech of François Legault, Premier of Québec, delivered on January 14, 2026. After seven years in power (2018-2026) and facing historically low poll numbers (11% support, 75% unfavorable), Legault announced his resignation "for the good of his party and especially for the good of Québec." This speech combines the announcement of his departure, expressions of gratitude, and a defense of his government's record. Your annotations will be used for computational analysis of political justification strategies, emotional framing, and policy legacy claims.

Annotate each sentence according to the dimensions below. Be precise and consistent. When multiple values apply within a dimension, include all relevant values.

**IMPORTANT**: You will receive each sentence with its preceding and following sentences as context. Only annotate the TARGET sentence (marked as such), using the context to inform your interpretation.

## Annotation Guidelines

1. **Be exhaustive for actors**: Capture all persons, organizations, and groups mentioned, including implicit references ("ceux qui ont pris le risque").

2. **Justification is central**: This is a resignation speech. Pay close attention to how Legault justifies both his departure AND his record. Most sentences likely serve a justificatory function.

3. **Distinguish defensive vs. proud tones**: The emotional register should capture whether Legault is defending against perceived criticism (DEFENSIVE) or celebrating achievements (PROUD).

4. **Policy specificity**: Use CAP-based categories precisely. "Maisons des aînés" is SENIORS, not just HEALTHCARE.

5. **Identity themes are key**: Québec nationalism and language/identity are recurring themes. Annotate these carefully.

6. **Legacy framing matters**: Legault is constructing his historical legacy. Note how he frames achievements as historic, pioneering, transformative, etc.

7. **Actors include implicit references**: "Le gouvernement fédéral" may be implicit criticism of Trudeau. "Ceux qui se sont embarqués" refers to early CAQ supporters.

8. **Empty values**: Use empty lists `[]` for actors and rhetorical_devices when none apply. Use `null` for optional fields when not applicable.

9. **Temporal complexity**: Many sentences look backward (achievements) while speaking in the present (resignation). Both temporalities may apply.

10. **Context window**: Use the preceding and following sentences to resolve ambiguities, but only annotate the TARGET sentence.

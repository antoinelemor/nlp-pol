# Annotation Prompt — Diplomatic Discourse Analysis (Presidential Speeches to Ambassadors)

## Context
You are annotating sentences from French presidential speeches addressed to ambassadors. These speeches articulate France's diplomatic doctrine, assess the international situation, and position France vis-à-vis other actors and global challenges. Your annotations will be used for computational analysis of diplomatic framing, policy content, and geopolitical positioning.

Annotate each sentence according to the dimensions below. Be precise and consistent. When multiple values apply within a dimension, include all relevant values.

---

## 1. SPEECH ACT (speech_act)
What is the primary communicative function of this sentence?

| Value | Definition | Example |
|-------|------------|---------|
| `STATING` | Factual description of a situation or state of affairs | "Les États-Unis sont une puissance établie." |
| `DIAGNOSING` | Causal analysis, interpretation of why something is happening | "Ce qui met à mal aujourd'hui l'économie européenne." |
| `DENOUNCING` | Explicit or implicit criticism of an actor, behavior, or situation | "Nous subissons une agressivité néocoloniale de certains." |
| `PROPOSING` | Announcing an action, initiative, policy, or solution | "Nous devons proposer des instruments nouveaux de maintien de la paix." |
| `EXHORTING` | Call to action, mobilization, rallying | "Ne cédez pas au défaitisme." |
| `REASSURING` | Valorizing achievements, encouraging, expressing confidence | "Nous avons énormément d'atouts." |
| `FRAMING` | Meta-commentary on the speech itself or how to interpret the situation | "Ce que je disais l'année dernière reste vrai." |
| `THANKING` | Expressions of gratitude | "Merci à vous, à vos équipes." |
| `WARNING` | Alerting to risks, dangers, negative consequences | "C'est le grand risque de l'ordre international." |

## Annotation Guidelines

1. **Be exhaustive for actors and stances**: Capture all actors mentioned and all positions expressed, even secondary ones.

2. **Distinguish explicit from implicit**: Use the `explicit` field carefully. "Les États-Unis se détournent de leurs alliés" is explicit. "Le premier actionnaire n'y croit plus" referring to the US is implicit.

3. **Policy content requires actionable substance**: Mere statements of principle ("nous croyons au multilatéralisme") are not policy content unless they specify an action or proposal.

4. **Valence vs. stance**: `actors.valence` is a simple +/- evaluation. `issue_stances.stance_content` captures the substance of the position.

5. **Specificity matters for policy**: Distinguish between concrete proposals ("create new peacekeeping structures with regional organizations") and aspirational goals ("defend multilateralism").

6. **Domain precision**: Use the most specific domain applicable. "Reform UN Security Council" is `SECURITY_COUNCIL`, not just `UN_REFORM`.

7. **When uncertain**: Prefer the more conservative annotation. If a frame is weakly present, do not include it.

8. **Empty values**: Use empty lists `[]` for actors, issue_stances, and rhetorical_devices when none apply. Use `null` for optional string/object fields when not applicable.

9. **Implicit references**: Be attentive to contemporary events being referenced without naming (Trump policies, specific crises, recent summits).

10. **Stance content brevity**: Keep stance_content under 15 words. Capture the essence, not the full nuance.
